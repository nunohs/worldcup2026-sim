The baseline model — team strength from raw goals scored and conceded — systematically overrated teams from confederations with weaker average schedules. Rebuilding the rating system as an iterative, opponent-weighted calculation (in the manner of Elo) corrected this and is the single largest driver of the accuracy gain above.

Method

Team strength
Attack and defence ratings are computed iteratively: a team's rating depends on the strength of its opponents, whose ratings in turn depend on theirs. Eight iterations to convergence. Recent matches (within 2 years) are weighted at full value; older results are discounted.
Match outcomes
Goals for each side are drawn independently from a Poisson distribution, parameterised by each team's attack rating against the opponent's defence rating. This produces realistic scorelines and allows upsets to emerge from the distribution rather than being hand-coded.
Adjustments
A structured manual layer accounts for confirmed injuries, managerial changes, and host-nation advantage (United States, Mexico, Canada), applied as bounded percentage adjustments to the statistical base rating.
Bracket
Results already decided in the live tournament are fixed inputs; only undecided matches are simulated. The bracket is chained through to the final using the official FIFA pairing structure.
